Development Journal

The Idea

For the Cutscene me and my group decided to make a fan made scene from the popular video game and film series Five Nights At Freddy’s. The idea was to have a simple scene that relates partially to the game and movie but taking our own twist by creating events that aren’t within these.

Five Nights at Freddy’s

Five Nights At Freddy’s is a game, book and movie series. The original game came out in August 2014 being the first installment into the Freddy’s universe. In the game you are a night guard looking to make a quick buck by looking after different sites from 12 to 6am. What isn’t told is that Animatronics will hunt you down and try to kill you (Five Nights at Freddy’s, s.d.). Making it a task to survive the 6 hours.

Figure 4 and 5 Shows the images of two main characters that we will be using for the cutscene, on the left is the Yellow Rabbit and the right the Puppet.

The story also delves into the history of freddy’s where its found out a character called William Afton (yellow rabbit (Spring Bonnie | Five Nights at Freddy’s Wiki | Fandom, s.d.)) has been killing children and stuffing them into suits to posses (William Afton (Film), 2025). In the second film there is a puppet called Marionette that protects children and defends them from harm (The Puppet, s.d.).

Using Excel, we filled out a Mocap planning sheet to plan what was gonna be shot, who was in the shots and how many shots needed to be taken. We also used a template provided to us to make a shot breakdown on word, this could also be printed for the shoot day.

MotionBuilder

To Import the individual character captures to unreal I needed to learn how to use MotionBuilder. It was pretty simple to learn, I had to add a cube to the First set of skeletons to create a mesh that unreal would recognise. After this was made it was a case of selecting the actor and saving the capture.

Figure 13 Shows an image of one of the character skeletons in MotionBuilder. Here you can see the process where we have to add a box to the rig so Unreal Engine recognises the mesh.

Figure 14 Shows a fully selected rig, this is what we had to do to save the selection in order to get specific character rigs for our cutscenes.

Animations and Audio

My audio was done in each sequencer i had made for the cutscene. In total I had 13 sequencers because the animations kept reorientating when layered, this meant I had to do some very quick scenes individully because the first person aspect showed alot of faults otherwise.

Issues that occured

Throughout this project many issues occured, the process of finding characters that were Unreal Engine comaptible was very difficualt, I had to learn how to use blender to de-rig characters and use mixamo to re-rig them. While this process was strightfroward it didnt always work and I must have spent 10 hours looking for 4 characters that could actually go into Unreal and use the mocap animations.

Later on in the project when I was making the sequences I also realised that despite adding censors to the hands during the shoot day, the finger capture did not work for any of the characters. This meant I had to become creative with how scenes were shot especially when almost the whole project was supposed to be shot in first person.

Figure 25 Shows the process I used to check the rigs, starting by finding a character on sketchfab, most of the bunny characters had feet very close together as shown. I imported the model into Blender, checked and deleted the rig then Exported it. From here I put the fbx into mixamo and used the auto rigging. In the last image you can see the issue I had with the feet being too close, unfortunately this was an issue for 80% of this specific character model on sketchfab. Gavin ended up stepping in and fixing this particular rig for us in maya.

Figure 26 Shows The 5 finger chapture set up we used on the child character. Its still unclear as to why none of the fingers for all the characters werent shot properly.
